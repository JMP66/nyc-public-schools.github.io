Statistics for NYC Public Middle Schools

To give you an introduction to the public middle schools in New York we have gathered some key statistics that will be presented below.
In general, information about the five boroughs of New York, Bronx, Brooklyn, Manhattan, Queens and Staten Island is given. However, the first plot shows the total number of schools in each school district and which borough each school district belongs to, so you can track your favorite school district throughout the rest of the plots.
For a better overview, the color used for each borough is consistent throughout the plots.
If you use mouse hover on the plots the exact numbers will be presented.

Overview

On the top plot in the left hand side you can see how many public middle schools there are in each of the 32 school districts. Furthermore, the colors indicate which borough each of the districts belong to. The plot below shows you the total number of schools in each borough. On the top plot in the right hand side you can see the total number of students enrolled in a public middle school in each of the boroughs. The plot below shows the average class size in each borough. This is calculated as the student-teacher ratio.
Staten Island only has one school district and hence, there are significantly fewer schools and total number of enrolled students in this borough. The average class sizes are very similar across the boroughs with the smallest being Bronx's on 12.5 and the largest being Queens's with 14.7.

Demographics

The top plot in the left hand side shows the average female/male distribution on the schools in each borough. On the right hand side the overall evolution of the female/male distribution from 2006-2018 is shown. The two middle plots shows the percentage of each etnicity in each borough and the overall evolution in the percentage of etnicities, respectively. These percentages are based on the number of students from each etnicity that is enrolled in the middle schools and is therefore not necessarily an indication of the general trend in each borough. The bottom plot in the left hand side shows the rental prices in each borough. We have divided the rental prices into three categories; <20$ per sqft, 20-30$ per sqft and >30$ per sqft , and show the distribution in each borough. The plot in the bottom right hand side shows the crimes per 100.000 citizen in each borough. Again, we have divided the number of crimes into three categories; < 30 felonies per 100.000, 30-60 felonies per 100.000 and >60 felonies per 100.000 and show the distribution in each borough.
The two bottom plots indicate that there is a negative correlation between rental prices and crime rates. Bronx is the borough with the lowest rental prices but is also the borough with the highest number of crimes.
